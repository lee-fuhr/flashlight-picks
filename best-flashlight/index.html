Frequently asked questions

What's the difference between lumens and throw distance?

Lumens measure total light output in every direction, the overall volume of light. Throw distance depends on beam intensity (candela), which is about how tightly the light is focused, not how much of it there is. A flood light can post big lumens with weak throw, while a focused thrower reaches far on a lower lumen count by concentrating the beam into a tight hotspot.

Should I get a rechargeable flashlight or one that takes disposable batteries?

Rechargeables (USB-C, 18650, or 21700 lithium-ion) generally deliver more power and cost less over time, but they need access to power to recharge. Disposable-battery lights are the better call for a glove box or emergency kit specifically because spare batteries store for years and swap in instantly with no power source needed. Plenty of people keep one of each.

What do IP ratings like IPX8 or IP68 actually mean on a flashlight?

The IP code has two digits: the first rates dust protection, the second rates water protection. 'IPX8' means dust wasn't tested (the X) but the light passed the highest common water test, typically full submersion. 'IP68' means both dust-tight and that same high-level water protection. Either one usually means the light survives full submersion; IP68 additionally guarantees it's dust-tight too.

What's the real difference between a tactical flashlight and an EDC flashlight?

Tactical lights are built for instant, reliable output under stress: simple tail-switch controls, quick strobe access, and rugged, sometimes strike-capable bezels. EDC (everyday carry) lights prioritize compact size and daily convenience, which is where you see extras like USB charging or a ramping brightness dial. Plenty of lights blur the line, but that design priority is the real differentiator.

Why does a flashlight's runtime drop so much on its brightest setting?

Brightness and runtime draw from the same battery capacity, so they trade off directly. It's common to see a light run only a couple hours on its highest turbo mode but 60-plus hours on a low setting, a roughly 30x runtime difference for a much smaller brightness difference. That's why most modern lights offer stepped or ramping brightness rather than one fixed output.
