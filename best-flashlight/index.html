Best for specific needs

Best for emergency preparedness

When the power goes out or you’re packing a go‑bag, you want something that runs on common batteries and is built to survive a drop. The Streamlight ProTac HL 5-X fits that bill perfectly: it takes both CR123A and 18650 cells, so you can scrounge batteries from cameras or vapes if needed. Its high output lights up a room, and the strobe works as a distress signal. Between the tough body and dual‑fuel flexibility, it’s the most practical emergency light you can grab right now. Our pick: Streamlight ProTac HL 5-X.

Best for color-critical work

If you’re a mechanic, electrician, or photographer, you need a light that shows wires and components in their true colors, not a muddy yellow mess. The Wurkkos FC11’s high‑CRI LED (90+ CRI) delivers vivid, accurate reds and greens, making it a standout for under‑hood repairs, circuit board inspections, or checking paint matches. It’s also budget‑friendly, so you don’t mind keeping one in the tool bag full time. Just don’t expect army‑spec toughness, and it’s fine for occasional drops. Our pick: Wurkkos FC11.

Best for keychain carry

When every ounce counts and you want a light that literally disappears until you need it, the Olight i5R EOS is the champ. It’s about the size of a pinky finger, runs on a rechargeable AA, and the magnetic charging puck makes topping off effortless. The beam is perfectly adequate for finding your keys, reading a menu in a dim restaurant, or lighting the path on a quick dog walk. Just be aware that the twist interface isn’t fast, and you only get one brightness level at a time. If that suits your needs, you’ll never leave home without it. Our pick: Olight i5R EOS.

Our verdict

At the end of the day, the ‘best flashlight’ is the one you actually have on you when the lights go out. For most of us, the Streamlight ProTac HL 5-X delivers the best overall package: robust, versatile, and backed by decades of reputation. If your budget is tighter, the Wurkkos FC11 is a surprisingly capable alternative that won’t leave you disappointed. And if you want something that will survive a nuclear winter and still work, the SureFire EDC2-DFT is the one to save up for. Pick the one that fits your pocket and your wallet, and you’ll be ready for anything.
